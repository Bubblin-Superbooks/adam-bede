“Ah, sir,” said Adam, for the first time feeling his own pain merged in sympathy for Arthur, “you and me'll often be thinking o' the same thing, when we're a long way off one another. I'll pray God to help you, as I pray him to help me.”

“But there's that sweet woman—that Dinah Morris,” Arthur said, pursuing his own thoughts and not knowing what had been the sense of Adam's words, “she says she shall stay with her to the very last moment—till she goes; and the poor thing clings to her as if she found some comfort in her.
