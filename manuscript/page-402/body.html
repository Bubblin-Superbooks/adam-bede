The word “gentleman” had a spell for Adam, and, as he often said, he “couldn't abide a fellow who thought he made himself fine by being coxy to's betters.” I must remind you again that Adam had the blood of the peasant in his veins, and that since he was in his prime half a century ago, you must expect some of his characteristics to be obsolete.
