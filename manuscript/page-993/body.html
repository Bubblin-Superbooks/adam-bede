Carroll, coming back, recalled Adam to the sense of his burden. He was to go into the study immediately. “I can't think what that strange person's come about,” the butler added, from mere incontinence of remark, as he preceded Adam to the door, “he's gone i' the dining-room. And master looks unaccountable—as if he was frightened.” Adam took no notice of the words: he could not care about other people's business. But when he entered the study and looked in Mr. Irwine's face, he felt in an instant that there was a new expression in it, strangely different from the warm friendliness it had always worn for him before.
