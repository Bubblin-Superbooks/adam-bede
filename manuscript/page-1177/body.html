I'll answer for it, you give Poyser many a good sermon.' The parson had thee there,” Mr. Poyser added, laughing unctuously. “I told Bartle Massey on it, an' he laughed too.”

“Yes, it's a small joke sets men laughing when they sit a-staring at one another with a pipe i' their mouths,” said Mrs. Poyser. “Give Bartle Massey his way and he'd have all the sharpness to himself. If the chaff-cutter had the making of us, we should all be straw, I reckon. Totty, my chicken, go upstairs to cousin Dinah, and see what she's doing, and give her a pretty kiss.”

This errand was devised for Totty as a means of checking certain threatening symptoms about the corners of the mouth; for Tommy, no longer expectant of cake, was lifting up his eyelids with his forefingers
