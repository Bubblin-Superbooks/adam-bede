When people asked Adam whose little lad he was, he had a sense of distinction as he answered, “I'm Thias Bede's lad.” He was quite sure everybody knew Thias Bede—didn't he make the wonderful pigeon-house at Broxton parsonage? Those were happy days, especially when Seth, who was three years the younger, began to go out working too, and Adam began to be a teacher as well as a learner.
