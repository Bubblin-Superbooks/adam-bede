Seth felt this dimly; he said to himself, “She's too good and holy for any man, let alone me,” and the words he had been summoning rushed back again before they had reached his lips. But another thought gave him courage: “There's no man could love her better and leave her freer to follow the Lord's work.” They had been silent for many minutes now, since they had done talking about Bessy Cranage; Dinah seemed almost to have forgotten Seth's presence, and her pace was becoming so much quicker that the sense of their being only a few minutes' walk from the yard-gates of the Hall Farm at last gave Seth courage to speak.

“You've quite made up your mind to go back to Snowfield o' Saturday, Dinah?”
