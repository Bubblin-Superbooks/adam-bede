Seth had said to Adam, “Can I help thee with anything in here to-night? I don't want to make a noise in the shop.”

“No, lad,” Adam answered, “there's nothing but what I must do myself. Thee'st got thy new book to read.”

And often, when Seth was quite unconscious, Adam, as he paused after drawing a line with his ruler, looked at his brother with a kind smile dawning in his eyes. He knew “th' lad liked to sit full o' thoughts he could give no account of; they'd never come t' anything, but they made him happy,” and in the last year or so, Adam had been getting more and more indulgent to Seth.
