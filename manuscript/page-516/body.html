And his savings had been so constantly drawn upon (besides the terrible sweep of paying for Seth's substitute in the militia that he had not enough money beforehand to furnish even a small cottage, and keep something in reserve against a rainy day. He had good hope that he should be “firmer on his legs” by and by; but he could not be satisfied with a vague confidence in his arm and brain; he must have definite plans, and set about them at once.
